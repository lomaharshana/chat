Sabudana Sandwich Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sabudana (tapioca pearls): Soaked for about 6-8 hours or overnight for best results.
  • 2 medium potatoes: Boiled and mashed.
  • 1/2 cup peanuts: Roasted and coarsely ground.
  • 2-3 green chilies: Finely chopped (adjust to taste).
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds.
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves: Finely chopped.
  • Salt to taste.
  • Lemon juice to taste.
  • 8 slices of bread (any type you prefer, but whole grain can add a nice texture).
  • Butter or ghee for toasting.
  • Optional: Slices of cucumber, tomato, and onion for added freshness and crunch.

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Sabudana Mixture:

    • Drain the soaked sabudana well to remove excess water.
    • In a bowl, mix the drained sabudana, mashed potatoes, ground peanuts, chopped green chilies, cumin seeds, coriander leaves, salt, and lemon juice. Combine everything well to form a thick mixture.

  2. Assemble the Sandwich:

    • Take a slice of bread and spread a generous amount of the sabudana mixture evenly on one side.
    • If using, place slices of cucumber, tomato, and onion over the mixture.
    • Cover with another slice of bread.

  3. Cook the Sandwich:

    • Heat a pan or sandwich maker over medium heat. Apply butter or ghee on the outside of the bread slices.
    • Place the sandwich in the pan or sandwich maker. Cook until the bread is golden brown and crispy on both sides. If using a pan, press down with a spatula and flip carefully to ensure even cooking.

  4. Serving:

    • Cut the cooked sandwiches into halves or quarters.
    • Serve hot with a side of mint chutney or ketchup for dipping.

Tips:

  • Soaking Time: Ensure sabudana is well soaked to achieve the right texture.
  • Adjust Heat: Green chilies can be adjusted according to your heat preference.
  • Variation: Feel free to add grated cheese inside the sandwich for a gooey twist.
